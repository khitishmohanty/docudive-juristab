Page 9Road Transport Legislation Amendment (Post-Crash Drug and Alcohol Testing) Bill 2025 [NSW]
Schedule 1   Amendment of Road Transport Act 2013 No 18
Division 4 Crashes
10 Definitions
In this division—
crash hospital patient  means a person who—
(a) attends at, or is admitted to, a hospital for examination or treatment
because of a crash, whether occurring in this jurisdiction or elsewhere,and
(b) is at least 15 years of age.
hospital  includes premises, including an institution or establishment,
prescribed by the statutory rules as being included for this definition.
11 Blood samples must be taken in hospitals from certain crash hospital patients
(1) An authorised sample taker attending to a crash hospital patient at a hospital
must take a sample of the patient’s blood for analysis if, at the time of thecrash, the patient was—
(a) a crash participant, or
(b) a pedestrian involved in the crash, or
(c) driving or riding a vehicle, other than a motor vehicle or bicycle,
involved in the crash, or
(d) driving or riding a horse involved in the crash.
(2) The sample must be taken—
(a) as soon as practicable, and
(b) whether or not the patient consents.
(3) The authorised sample taker is not required to take the sample if—
(a) a sample of the patient’s blood has already been taken in accordance
with this clause, or
(b) the authorised sample taker has been informed by a police officer, or has
reasonable grounds to believe, that the sample is required to be taken forthe purposes of clause 12.
(4) A sample taken under this clause may be used for the purpose of conducting
an alcohol analysis of the blood.
(5) A sample taken from a patient under this clause in relation to a crash may be
used for the purpose of conducting a drug analysis of the blood if—
(a) the patient was a crash participant, and
(b) a police officer has reasonable grounds to believe the crash resulted
in—
(i) the death of a person, or
(ii) grievous bodily harm to a person.
Note 1— Part 4 provides for the procedures in relation to the taking and analysis of
samples taken under this clause.
Note 2— Part 5 provides for the admissibility of evidence of the presence and
concentration of alcohol and drugs in blood samples taken under this clause andanalysed in accordance with Part 4.
